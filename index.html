Enough is Enough

Our public district hospital, Olympic Medical Center (OMC), is on the brink of closure and financial insolvency.

Indisputable Facts:

  • $72 million dollars “lost” from 2022 to 2025
  • 5 days cash on hand at the end of 2023 per an official state audit report
  • $30 million dollars currently short of meeting bond obligations
  • 70+ quality citations from the Washington Department of Health
  • 4 felony convictions against an emergency room physician convicted of sexual assault and rape in 2024
  • $1.4 million lawsuit settlement for failing to pay employees in violation of the Fair Labor Standards Act and state requirements in 2024
  • 142% increase in property taxes passed by voters in 2024 to support OMC
  • $30 million dollars of debt resulting in bills and invoices currently going unpaid
  • $9 million dollars lost moving forward due to mismanaging of 340B drug discounts
  • 286 minutes waiting on average for emergency room visit (compared to 161 minutes on average in other WA hospitals)
  • 6 visits from state auditors since February 2025 for repeated and unaddressed quality and safety problems
  • 1 immediate jeopardy citation from the Washington Department of Health in April 2025 threatening closure
  • 3 letters from the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid since April 2025 that have threatened to terminate reimbursement
  • 3 C-suite members that have resigned in disgrace since July 2025

The question for the publicly elected Board of Commissioners is: How is this oversight?

Yet the publicly elected Board of Commissioners continues to deflect responsibility - hiring an interim CEO at the cost of $50,000 per month, spending recklessly and wastefully on additional consultants at the cost of $250,000 for 6 weeks, and pointing to a partnership with the University of Washington, which is exploratory, preliminary, and years away from completion (if it even happens) - as “success.”

Meanwhile, the threat of mass layoffs, shuttering of services, and financial collapse continues to loom.

Conclusion: The Board of Commissioners has utterly failed in its fiduciary responsibility to provide the necessary oversight of OMC on every level - from ensuring that the public has a safe, quality hospital to providing financial stewardship - leaving the patients, employees, and entire community to suffer the consequences.

The citizens of Clallam County deserve better. They deserve accountability. Vote out the Board of Commissioners.
